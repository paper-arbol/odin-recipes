Lentil Soup

Description

Lentil soup is a soup based on lentils; it may be vegetarian or include meat, and may use brown, red, yellow, green or black lentils, with or without the husk. Dehulled yellow and red lentils disintegrate in cooking, making a thick soup. It is a staple food throughout Europe, Latin America and the Middle East.

Ingredients

  • 2l vegetable stock
  • 150g red lentils
  • 6 carrots, chopped
  • 2 medium leeks, chopped
  • parsley to serve

Steps

  1. Heat the stock in a large pan and add the lentils. Bring to the boil and allow the lentils to soften for a few minutes.
  2. Add the carrots and leeks and season (don't add salt if you use ham stock as it will make it too salty). Bring to the boil, then reduce the heat, cover and simmer for 45-60 mins until the lentils have broken down. Scatter over the parsley and serve with buttered bread, if you like.
